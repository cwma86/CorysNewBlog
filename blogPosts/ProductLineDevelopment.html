Product Line Software Development

Introduction

Previously I worked for a company that developed software that was being deployed to around 50 different environments across several separate contracts for 2 primary customers. These contracts focused on expanding our software to new environments, adding new functionality, and sustainment/maintenance of existing deployments. When I first joined the company, software development teams were structured to focus on individual contracts and were responsible for updates across our full baseline. Over time, we eventually transitioned to separating our baseline into several different products/core-assets and structuring teams so that they focused on developing for the products instead of the contracts. Over the next couple sections I want to touch on the issues we discovered with contract based development, and success we had transitioning to product line based approach.

 

 

Issues with contract based development teams

         Implementation focuses on the needs of the contract not long term success of the baseline

o   Branching issues

o   Merge conflicts

o   To many stakeholders at interfaces

         Siloed developers that only understand the use cases of their system

o   Reduced quality due to developers breaking functionality they didnt know existed

Successes of product line teams

         Developers have smaller focus area and can get spun up faster

o   Software is complex, and the smaller you scope work the less risk involved with regression

o   Psychologically less overwhelming for developers to be responsible for 10% of the code base instead of 100%

         Developers learn all use cases of their software for more stable long term development

o   Reduction in bugs related to regression of legacy functionality

         Development teams are the primary stakeholders of their software module

o   Since teams need to maintain their component across various users, they are more inclined to make changes that maintain legacy functionality, while also focusing on ease of adding additional functionality in the future.

Downfalls of product line teams

         Developers get siloed into a single areas of the baselines

o   Increased difficulty of integration due to less full stack developers

         Teams have to juggle priorities from various contracts

o   Program managers always think there project is highest priority

         Reliance on better cross team communications

o   Cross team collaboration was often found to be difficult, and extra focus was needed to ensure both teams were developing components that aligned with each other.

         CI/CD, testing, and integration were performed on a more team by team basis and formal process and standardization was difficult to unify between all teams
